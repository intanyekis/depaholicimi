Tyler Hamilton (born March 1, 1971, Marblehead, Massachusetts) is a former American professional road bicycle racer who competed in the Tour de France and the Tour de France Prologue. He has the distinction of being the only American to have won one of the Five Monuments of Cycling. Hamilton began his professional cycling career in 1995 as a member of the United States Postal Service cycling team. He was a teammate of Lance Armstrong during the Tour de France in 1999, 2000, and 2001, during which Armstrong won the Yellow Jersey for the third time. Hamilton had appearances in the Summer Olympics in 2000 and 2004. 

In 2004, he earned a gold medal in the time trial at the World Championships. Despite the fact that the initial doping test returned a positive result, no doping violation could be proved since the backup sample was frozen. When Hamilton failed further drug tests during the 2004 Vuelta a Espanha, he was banned from cycling for two years. Hamilton returned to the racing scene after his ban and won the National Road Race Championship in 2008. The next year, Hamilton failed a drug test for the second time and was banned for eight years, essentially forcing him to retire. Earlier this year, he was summoned to testify before a grand jury about the usage of performance-enhancing substances during his bicycle racing career. Hamilton confessed in May 2011 that he had taken prohibited drugs during a tournament and that he had surrendered his gold medal. 

This is detailed in his book The Secret Race: Inside the Hidden World of the Tour de France: Doping, Cover-ups, and Winning at Any Cost (published in 2012), which recounts his doping methods and experiences while competing in the sport of cycling. Hamilton’s gold medal from the 2004 Olympics was taken from him by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) on August 10, 2012.
